Pro Tips

Get More from Your Lists

Simple habits that make your lists smarter and your life easier.

Plan Your Grocery List by Aisle

Group related grocery items together when building your list — produce, dairy, meats, frozen. It cuts your shopping time significantly and reduces backtracking around the store.

Use Priority Levels for To-Do Lists

Mark your most important tasks as "High Priority" so they stand out visually. Focus on those first each morning before moving to normal and low-priority tasks.

Share Shopping Lists with Family

Use the share button to send your shopping list to a family member. They can open it in their browser and you can divide the shopping without endless back-and-forth messages.

Set Due Dates on All Tasks

Tasks without deadlines tend to stay undone. Even if the deadline is flexible, adding a due date creates accountability and keeps important work visible in your list view.

Keep a Master Checklist Template

Create a reusable packing checklist for trips or a routine checklist for recurring events. After completing it, use "Clear Done" to reset and reuse the same list next time.

Export Lists Before Big Events

Before a shopping trip or important event, use the Export button to download your list as a .txt file. Great as a printed backup when you won't have phone access.
