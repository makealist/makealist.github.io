    Tips For Making A List You'll Actually Use

    Any list maker can hold text. The difference between a list that gets finished and one that gets abandoned halfway usually comes down to a few small habits. Here's what tends to work.

    Write it down the moment you think of it

    The whole point of an online to do list maker is that it's always within reach. Add an item the second it crosses your mind instead of trying to hold it in memory until later — that's how things get forgotten in the first place.

    Keep one list per purpose

    A single sprawling list mixing groceries, work tasks, and packing items gets confusing fast. Run a separate grocery list maker session for the store and a different to do list maker session for work — name each one so you can tell them apart at a glance.

    Check things off as you go, not after

    Marking an item done the instant it's finished keeps the checklist online honest and gives an accurate read on what's actually left, rather than a guess made at the end of the day.

    Clear finished lists instead of hoarding them

    Once a checklist maker session has done its job, clear it. Keeping every completed list around just adds noise — the goal of an online list is speed, not an archive of everything you've ever needed to do.
