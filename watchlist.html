How To Use

HOW TO BUILD YOUR WATCH LIST

A great watch list is more than a simple collection of titles — it's your personal entertainment roadmap. MakeAList's Watch List Tracker gives you three curated lists to keep everything organized and easy to browse.

1

Add a Title

Type the movie, TV show, anime, or video title in the input field. Select the media type, add the release year, and optionally assign a star rating. Hit Add to save it instantly.

2

Choose a Status

Use the tabs to switch between your three lists: Want to Watch for your backlog, Watching for currently active titles, and Finished for everything you've completed.

3

Move Between Lists

Click the move buttons on any card to shift a title between statuses. Started watching something? Move it from Want to Watch → Watching. Finished? Move it to Finished.

4

Add Notes & Ratings

Click the Notes button on any card to add personal thoughts, episode counts, or recommendations. Assign star ratings to easily recall your favorites later.

About

THE SMARTER WATCH LIST

We've all been there — someone recommends a show, you say "I'll watch it later," and then forget it completely. Or you finish a great series and can't remember what you were watching before. A dedicated watch list solves all of that.

MakeAList's Watch List Tracker is designed for movie fans, TV series binge-watchers, anime enthusiasts, and casual YouTube viewers alike. It works equally well as a movie watch list, a TV show tracker, or an anime list. The three-column system — Want to Watch, Watching, and Finished — mirrors how most people naturally think about media consumption.

Everything is saved automatically in your browser. No account, no subscription, no app to download. Just open the page and your entire watch list is exactly where you left it. Use the search bar to find any title instantly, or sort your lists by newest, oldest, alphabetical, or top-rated.

Whether you're tracking 10 titles or 500, the Watch List Tracker scales beautifully on any screen — desktop, tablet, or mobile. It's the simplest, fastest way to never lose track of what to watch next.
